Samosa

Samosa is a popular Indian snack with a crispy outer layer and spicy potato filling.It is often enjoyed hot with chutney or tea.

start cooking See Recipe

Ingredients

    For the Dough
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 4 tbsp oil or ghee
  • ½ tsp carom seeds (ajwain)
  • ½ tsp salt
  • ½ cup water (adjust as needed)
    For the Filling
  • 3 medium potatoes (boiled & mashed)
  • ½ cup green peas (boiled)
  • 1–2 green chilies (finely chopped)
  • 1 tsp ginger (grated)
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds (jeera)
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • ½ tsp garam masala
  • ½ tsp turmeric powder
  • ½ tsp red chili powder
  • 1 tsp amchur powder (dry mango powder) or lemon juice
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tbsp oil for cooking filling
    For Frying
  • Oil (enough for deep frying)

instructions

    Step 1: Prepare the Dough
  • In a mixing bowl, add flour, ajwain, and salt. Add oil/ghee and mix well until the flour becomes crumbly. Gradually add water and knead into a firm dough. Cover with a damp cloth and let it rest for 30 minutes.
    Step 2: Make the Filling
  • Heat 2 tbsp oil in a pan. Add cumin seeds and let them crackle. Add ginger and green chilies, sauté for 1 minute. Add boiled peas, stir for 2 minutes. Add boiled & mashed potatoes. Add coriander powder, garam masala, turmeric, red chili powder, and salt. Mix well and cook for 3–4 minutes. Add amchur powder or lemon juice, mix, and turn off the heat. Let the filling cool completely.
    Step 3: Shape the Samosas
  • Divide the dough into equal lemon-sized balls. Roll one ball into an oval shape (~6 inches long). Cut it in half to make two semi-circles. Take one semi-circle, fold into a cone shape, and seal the edge using a little water. Fill the cone with 2–3 tbsp of potato-pea filling. Seal the open edge by pressing tightly. Repeat for all.
    Step 4: Fry the Samosas
  • Heat oil on medium-low heat (not too hot or samosas will bubble and burn outside). Fry samosas in batches until golden brown and crisp (about 8–10 minutes per batch). Remove onto paper towels to drain excess oil.

preparation time per serving

Dough making: 10 minutes, Resting time: 30 minutes, Filling preparation: 15 minutes, Shaping: 20 minutes, Frying: 20 minutes,

Total time: ~1 hour 35 minutes

Per serving (2 samosas): ~15 minutes active work

Golgappe

Golgappe are tiny crispy shells filled with spicy, tangy water and potato mix — little bombs of happiness that burst with flavor in your mouth

start cooking See Recipe

Ingredients

    For the Puri
  • 1 cup fine semolina (sooji/rava)
  • 2 tbsp all-purpose flour (maida)
  • A pinch of salt
  • ¼ cup water (as needed, for stiff dough)
  • Oil (for deep frying)
    For the Spicy Water (Pani)
  • 1 cup fresh coriander leaves
  • ½ cup mint leaves
  • 2–3 green chilies
  • 1-inch piece ginger
  • 1 tsp roasted cumin powder
  • 1 tsp black salt (kala namak)
  • ½ tsp regular salt
  • 2 tbsp tamarind pulp
  • 3–4 cups chilled water
    For the Filling
  • 3–4 medium potatoes (boiled & mashed)
  • ½ cup boiled black chickpeas (optional)
  • 1 tsp chaat masala
  • ½ tsp red chili powder
  • Salt to taste

instructions

    Salt to taste
  • In a bowl, mix sooji, maida, and salt. Gradually add water and knead into a tight, stiff dough. Cover and rest for 20–25 minutes. Roll out the dough thinly and cut small circles (about coin-sized). Heat oil on medium flame and fry puris until they puff up and turn golden. Drain on paper towels and let them cool until crisp.
    Step 2: Make the Spicy Water (Pani)
  • Blend coriander, mint, green chilies, and ginger into a smooth paste with a little water. Transfer to a large bowl. Add roasted cumin powder, black salt, regular salt, tamarind pulp, and mix. Add 3–4 cups of chilled water and stir well. Adjust spices to taste. Chill until serving.
    Step 3: Prepare the Filling
  • In a bowl, add boiled potatoes and mash well. Mix with boiled chickpeas (if using), chaat masala, red chili powder, and salt. Keep aside.
    Step 4: Assemble the Golgappe
  • Take one crispy puri and gently crack the top. Add 1 spoon of potato-chickpea filling. Dip into the spicy tangy water (pani) or pour it in. Eat immediately for best taste!

preparation time per serving

Puri dough & rolling: 25 minutes, Resting time: 20 minutes, Frying puris: 20 minutes, Making pani: 10 minutes, Filling preparation: 10 minutes,

Total time: ~1 hour 25 minutes

Per serving (6–8 puris): ~10 minutes active
